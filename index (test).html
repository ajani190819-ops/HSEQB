Help & Tutorial

Getting Started

Open the sidebar and work through each section in order: Session → Players → Teams → Categories. Once configured, use the Record Answer panel to log answers in real time. All data syncs automatically across all connected devices via Firebase.

Sessions

Each match is a Session. Create one with a descriptive name before recording answers. Use Load Session to switch to a previous match. Sessions can be marked as excluded from analytics — excluded sessions are dimmed and ignored in all Stats calculations.

Players & Teams

Add players in the Players section, then create teams in Teams and assign players to them. Only players assigned to a team are selectable when recording answers. Players are stored in a global pool and are available across all sessions.

Recording Answers

Select a Player, then a Point Type, then a Category (optional for Neg), then press Record Answer. Use the answer log to delete mistakes.

Toss-up+10Standard correct answer on a toss-up

Toss-Ups Heard Counter

The Toss-Ups Heard counter tracks how many toss-up questions have been read to the team. It increments automatically on every Power, Toss-up, or Neg (a Neg immediately followed by a correct answer counts as one question). Use the +/− buttons to correct it manually. This value is required for all per-player efficiency metrics.

Stats (Analytics)

Toggle to the Stats view using the switch in the header. Stats are aggregated across all non-excluded sessions and organized into four tabs:

  • Overview — session totals, category strengths & weaknesses, top performers
  • Players — ranked leaderboard with sortable columns; expand any player for full metrics, or click their name to open the detail modal
  • Categories — answer counts and point totals by subject area
  • Team Compositions — per-team stats for every session; click to expand

Player Metrics

Expand any player in the Players tab to see their individual metrics. All metrics require toss-ups heard data to calculate.

  • Potential Points — total points if every toss-up win had earned a full bonus (15P + 40T − 5N)
  • Production Rate — potential points per toss-up heard; the primary measure of per-opportunity impact
  • Buzz Volume — percentage of toss-ups the player buzzed on (optional — can be disabled in Skill Score Settings)
  • Confidence — net powered buzzes per toss-up heard: 100 × (Powers − Negs) / TU Heard. Positive means the player is powering more than they're negging; negative means negs are outweighing powers
  • Skill Score — composite 0–100 score where 50 = exactly team average. See below.

Skill Score

Skill Score compares each player to the team average across weighted metrics. A score of 50 means exactly average; 100 means twice the team average across all metrics.

Formula: each metric is normalized as player value / team average, then combined as a weighted sum and scaled so 1.0 (= average) maps to 50.

Default weights are mathematically derived from realistic IHSA match data:

  • Buzz on: Production Rate 50% · Buzz Volume 33% · Confidence 17%
  • Buzz off: Production Rate 75% · Confidence 25%

Players with fewer buzz answers than the low-data threshold are flagged and excluded from group averages by default. You can manually include or exclude flagged players in the Low-Data Players panel. Inclusion overrides sync globally across all devices and persist across sessions.

Leaderboard Sorting

Use the sort pills above the player list to rank by Points, Skill Score, Production Rate, Confidence, Powers, or Negs (fewest first). Buzz Volume is also available as a sort option when buzz is enabled. Ties in Points are broken by most Powers, then fewest Negs.

Export & Import

Export to CSV for spreadsheets or JSON to back up and re-import a full session. Both options are in the Data section of the sidebar.

What Syncs Globally

The following are stored in Firebase and sync instantly across all connected devices:

  • All session data (answers, scores, toss-ups heard)
  • The global player pool
  • Skill Score settings (threshold, weights, buzz on/off, auto weights toggle)
  • Low-data player inclusion/exclusion overrides

The following are stored on your device only and do not sync: Dark Mode, accent color, scrollbar visibility, and sidebar state.
