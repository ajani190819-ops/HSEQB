Help & Tutorial

Getting Started

Open the sidebar and work through each section in order:Session → Players → Teams → Categories. Once configured, use the Record Answer panel to log answers in real time. All data syncs automatically across all connected devices via Firebase.

Sessions

Each match is a Session. Create one with a descriptive name before recording answers. Use Load Session to switch to a previous match. Sessions can be marked as excluded from analytics — excluded sessions are dimmed and ignored in all Stats calculations.

Players & Teams

Add players in the Players section, then create teams in Teams and assign players to them. Only players assigned to a team are selectable when recording answers. Players are stored in a global pool and are available across all sessions.

Recording Answers

Select a Player, then a Point Type, then a Category (optional for Neg), then press Record Answer. Use the answer log to delete mistakes.

Toss-Ups Heard Counter

The Toss-Ups Heard counter tracks how many toss-up questions have been read to the team. It increments automatically on every Power, Toss-up, or Neg (a Neg immediately followed by a correct answer counts as one question). Use the +/− buttons to correct it manually. This value is required for all per-player efficiency metrics.

Stats (Analytics)

Toggle to the Stats view using the switch in the header. Stats are aggregated across all non-excluded sessions and organized into four tabs:

  • Overview — totals, category breakdowns, top performers
  • Players — ranked leaderboard; expand for metrics or click name for detail
  • Categories — answer counts and points by subject
  • Team Compositions — per-team stats per session

Player Metrics

Expand any player in the Players tab to see their individual metrics. The per-TU-heard stats require toss-ups heard data.

  • Points — total points across all sessions
  • Toss-ups — total correct toss-up answers (powered + regular); ⚡︎ = answered before power mark for +15 instead of +10
  • Actual Pts / 20 TU — earned points per 20 toss-ups heard: pts / TH × 20
  • TU Efficiency — pts per correct answer: (15P + 10T) / (P+T). Range 10–15. Blind to negs, misses, and TU heard.
  • Accuracy — correct buzz rate: (P+T) / (P+T+N+M) × 100. Blind to TU heard and power/normal distinction.
  • Conversion Rate — correct answers per TU heard: (P+T) / TH × 100. Blind to negs, misses, and power/normal distinction.
  • Skill Score — TU Efficiency + Accuracy, normalized to team avg = 100
  • Contribution Score — Conversion Rate, normalized to team avg = 100
  • Overall Score — 50/50 average of Skill and Contribution, normalized to team avg = 100

Score System

All three scores are relative to the team — the team average always equals 100. They are built from three independent components:

  • TU Efficiency (15P+10T)/(P+T) — how valuable your correct answers are. A player who powers consistently will score higher here. Denominator is correct answers only — negs and misses are invisible.
  • Accuracy (P+T)/(P+T+N+M)×100 — how disciplined your buzzing is. Penalizes negging and missing. Denominator is all buzz attempts — TU heard is invisible.
  • Conversion Rate (P+T)/TH×100 — how often you convert a heard tossup into a correct answer for your team. Captures contribution volume. Denominator is TU heard — negs and misses are invisible.

Skill Score = average of TU Efficiency + Accuracy. Measures how good you are per opportunity.

Contribution Score = Conversion Rate. Measures how much you contribute to the team per tossup heard.

Overall Score = 50/50 Skill + Contribution. Balances individual quality with team contribution.

Each component is regression-adjusted using TU Heard — players with more TU heard have their actual numbers trusted more, while low-data players are pulled toward the group mean. A player at 150 is 50% above average; a player at 50 is half the team average.

Skill = z(TU Efficiency) + z(Accuracy) / 2  ·  Contribution = z(Conversion Rate)  ·  Overall = (Skill + Contribution) / 2

Players below the low-data threshold are excluded from scoring by default. Toggle inclusion in the Low-Data Players panel.

Radar Chart

Shows all three score components — TU Efficiency, Accuracy, and Conversion Rate — for the selected player vs the team average. TU Efficiency is normalized from its 10–15 range to 0–100 for display. Raw values are shown in point labels and tooltips.

Leaderboard Sorting

Sort by Points, Overall Score, Pts / 20 TU, Actual Pts / 20 TU, TU Efficiency, Accuracy, Powers, Negs (fewest first), or A–Z. Ties in Points are broken by most Powers, then fewest Negs.

Export & Import

Export to CSV for spreadsheets or JSON to back up and re-import a full session. Both options are in the Data section of the sidebar.

What Syncs Globally

The following are stored in Firebase and sync instantly across all connected devices:

  • All session data (answers, scores, toss-ups heard)
  • Global player pool
  • Skill Score threshold
  • Low-data player overrides

The following are stored on your device only and do not sync:Dark Mode, accent color, scrollbar visibility, and sidebar state.

Version & Updates

This app uses a semantic version system. The display version (e.g., 1.5.1) is set by the admin team and appears in the header. Updates are only checked if you're running a local copy of the file — users on the GitHub Pages site or cloud deployments don't see update prompts.
